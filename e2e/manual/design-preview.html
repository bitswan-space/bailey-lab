CH 01 Ship changes

Sync & Deploy

From a working copy to a healthy deployment — with a security check standing between you and production.

Live capture
▣Sync & Deploy → Checks tab, CVEs of the image this deploy would build

One button does the careful thing: it commits your work-in-progress, rebases onto main, fast-forwards, and rolls out to development — tracking the single deploy it started instead of racing a second one.

Before you promote, the Checks tab shows the exact CVEs of the image this deploy would build. Click any CVE to open the advisory. Mark what's out of scope — into your source tree, where review can see it.

How to ship a change

1
Open Sync & Deploy on your business process.
2
Review the diff and the Checks tab.
3
Press Sync & Deploy — development goes Healthy.
4
Promote dev → staging → production when you're ready.
Why it matters

The check happens on the image that will actually run — not last week's scan. Security is a step in the flow, not a gate someone forgets.

Bailey is best practice — what the standards ask of you, and how this does it
  • ISO/IEC 27001A.8.8
    Management of technical vulnerabilities. You must obtain timely information about vulnerabilities in the software you run and evaluate your exposure. The Checks tab scans the exact image before it ships and links each CVE to its advisory.
  • NIS2Art. 21(2)(d) & (e)
    Supply-chain security & secure acquisition. Vulnerability handling and the security of your software supply chain are explicit duties. Bitswan records waivers in your source tree, so every accepted risk is versioned and reviewable.
  • DORAArt. 8–9
    ICT risk identification & protection. Continuously identify ICT-supported risks before deployment. Pre-deploy scanning makes that identification automatic and auditable.

CH 02 Sleep at night

Rehearse & Restore

A backup you've never restored is a rumor. Bitswan makes the rehearsal a routine — and the real thing a single swap.

Live capture
▣Disaster Recovery · the three-slot state diagram with Production / DR / Idle tags

Restore a production backup into the DR slot — never onto live production. Open it, click around, confirm it's whole. Only the backup actually loaded into DR can be marked recovery-tested — because you can only vouch for what you've seen.

When you need to go live, the Restore pill performs an ingress cutover: the -production host repoints to the verified slot. No data migration, no redeploy, no downtime.

How to run a recovery drill

1
In Production → Backups, take a snapshot.
2
Go to Disaster Recovery → Rehearse & restore.
3
Restore into DR, then verify and Mark recovery-tested.
4
Use the Restore pill to swap DR live when needed.

Bailey is best practice — what the standards ask of you, and how this does it
  • DORAArt. 11 & 12
    Response, recovery & backup, restoration and testing. You must maintain backup procedures and regularly test your ability to restore. Rehearse & Restore turns that test into a routine and records when it last passed.
  • ISO/IEC 27001A.8.13 / A.5.30
    Information backup & ICT readiness for continuity. Backups must be maintained and verified by restoration. A recovery test is only valid against the backup actually loaded into DR.
  • NIS2Art. 21(2)(c)
    Business continuity & crisis management. Backup management and disaster recovery are required measures. A zero-downtime swap means recovery doesn't cost you an outage.
