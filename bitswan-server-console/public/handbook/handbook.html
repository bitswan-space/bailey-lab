CH 10Sleep at night

Rehearse & restore

A backup you’ve never restored is a rumor. Bitswan makes the rehearsal a routine — and the real thing a single swap.

Live capture
Disaster Recovery · backup loaded into DR, recovery-tested

Restore a production backup into the DR slot — never onto live production. Open it, confirm it’s whole. Only the backup actually loaded into DR can be marked recovery-tested.

When you must go live, the Restore pill performs an ingress cutover: -production repoints to the verified slot. No data migration, no redeploy, no downtime.

How to

Take a production snapshot.
2
Go to Disaster Recovery → Rehearse & restore.
3
Restore into DR, verify, Mark recovery-tested.

Use the Restore pill to swap live when needed.

CH 12Control the edges

Firewall & data processing

An egress allow-list with a GDPR data-processing record for every external host — approval workflow, versioning and the DPA on file.

Live capture
Firewall · egress allow-list with GDPR records

Meridian’s invoice flow may reach its vendor portals, the Czech business register and a payment gateway — and nothing else. Each allowed host carries a GDPR data-processing record: the purpose, whether personal data flows to that recipient, and the signed Data Processing Agreement on file.

Egress is default-deny. A new destination can’t go live until someone completes its processing record — so your Article 30 register builds itself as the system is operated, instead of being reconstructed under audit pressure.

How to

1
Open a stage → Firewall.
2
Review hosts waiting for approval.
3
Complete the GDPR data-processing record (purpose, personal data, lawful basis).
4
Attach the processor’s DPA and approve — the host is recorded and allowed.
GDPR, by construction

Every external recipient of data is recorded with its purpose and contract at the moment it’s allowed. The record of processing activities (Art. 30) and the processor agreements (Art. 28) are a by-product of running the system — not a spreadsheet someone keeps separately.

Bailey is best practice — what the standards ask of you, and how this does it
  • GDPRArt. 30
    Records of processing activities. Each external recipient is logged with its purpose and whether personal data is transferred — the register maintained automatically.
  • GDPRArt. 28
    Processor obligations. A Data Processing Agreement is stored on file for every host that processes personal data before egress is allowed.
  • NIS2Art. 21(2)(a)
    Risk-analysis & network security policies. Default-deny egress with reviewed, documented exceptions.
  • ISO/IEC 27001A.8.20–8.21
    Network & network-service security. Outbound connections are controlled per service.
